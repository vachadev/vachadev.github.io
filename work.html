Our Work

Vacha works together to develop strategies, mobilize resources and raise awareness to combat systemic gender and racial inequality at the community level, forging a better quality of life for all. Learn how you can join us.

Girls’ Resource Center

Vacha creates safe spaces in their centres for adolescent girls and helps them acquire 21st-century life skills such as learning how to use computers and conversing in English and spreading awareness about accessing area resources.

Reach & Impact

This year witnessed a series of sessions, workshops, campaigns and exposure visits on life skills and gender. Older girls were trained in goal setting, critical thinking and the Right to Information while the younger girls were educated on pubertal changes, the social relevance of menstruation and self-expression techniques among others.

Exposure visits are essential for the overall growth of our program participants as these visits provide exposure to the world outside their communities. This year, our program participants visited the Nehru Science Centre, a post office, Public parks and gardens, a Bank, the Zoo, the Agricultural Produce Market Committee, a Police Station, a Health post and a Solid Waste Management unit which was beneficial in helping the program participants understand different systems.

With the addition of new centers this year, we have been able to reach out to 1408 girls and 313 boys through direct interventions like regular after-school sessions, workshops, exposure visits.

The Time management session helped the program participants to effectively plan and manage their time.

Theatre and Music workshop helped the program participants learn voice modulation, acting and ways to create a script for street play on the different issues.

First Aid Training helps younger girls understand the contents of the First Aid box and how to provide immediate medical assistance.

Self-Expression sessions were held to ensure girls were able to express themselves openly.

Discussion on newspaper articles especially the ones featuring women and girls issues were discussed.

Youth Empowerment

Vacha's youth empowerment programme ensures effective participation of youth in community life by building their capacity and increasing social awareness we encourage older girls in the centre to become peer mentors and role models for younger girls as well as new participants.

Youth Council

Participants in the age group of 13—17 years who have been regular and most active have been elected by others to become members of the Youth Council across 13 centers. Last year, the councils had joint meetings to discuss the challenges faced by the community and devised mitigation strategies.

Mentorship

Mentorship of younger program participants by older girls is critical to generate cross-learning and experience sharing to work in the community. These older girls have a deep understanding of Vacha's work and therefore ideal mentors for the younger program participants. Mentorship also encourages community youth to own projects and utilize skills imparted during our capacity-building sessions. Younger program participants feel empowered to undertake community action with the support of their mentors.

Last year, we had 147 mentors working with our younger program participants in driving community events.

Our youth group from the Kalyan community decided to beautify the school by organising a cleanliness drive along with a tree plantation drive and successfully planted 23 samplings across 5 schools.

Youth groups from Santacruz organised a signature campaign to request the Metro Authority to provide concessions to college students as the daily travel through Metro is expensive.

Youth Group in Andheri approached the local police to increase patrolling in their community due to increasing cases of sexual harassment.

Research & Training

Vacha regularly conducts training with young participants to develop their understanding of gender constructs and generate awareness on gender issues. The objective of this training is to help the participants acquire and apply gender perspectives in their personal and professional lives.

Reach & Impact

This year, Vacha conducted a two-day training for nine team members of the Just for Kicks Organisation. The training highlighted issues of Sex and Gender, Patriarchy, Menstruation and what it means to be an Adolescent Girl. A Power Walk was organized to illuminate how patriarchy, economic, social, cultural and political structures create inequalities. The participants appreciated the examples of how structures like Family, Marriage, Religious practices, Economy perpetuate patriarchy and how women are impacted in the process. In partnership with Friedrich-Ebert-Stiftung (FES), we undertook three pieces of training with non-profit organization teams, college students and students pursuing bachelor’s degrees in education/ teacher training to build their capacities on advanced gender concepts. The training received an overwhelming response from the participants who narrated their life experiences from the gender purview. We conducted similar training for college students and students pursuing bachelors’ degrees in education/ teacher along with fairs.

In total, 138 women and 95 men underwent this training across organisations and through the gender-fair, participants were able to reach out to 671 women and 455 men.

Participants shared that they were excited to be a part of the decision-making and planning phase along with the implementation phase.

Some of the male participants shared that they never understood the inequalities of women in their house and always viewed things from a male perspective.

Participants expressed their awareness of gender issues and their relevance in their personal lives.

Awareness Building

As a part of awareness building, social action projects are carried out in the communities that aim at addressing community issues affecting girls’ rights as well as increase their participation in civic community issues. This year, adolescents and youth groups from Vacha implemented the action based on the research study carried out last year in the community.

Right to Safety

The lack of street lights in bastis has always been a matter of safety concern for girls in the community. Girls from our Andheri community decided to work on this issue and filed an application under the Right to Information Act to know about the number of street lights in their area and how many of them were functional.

Access to Clean Toilets

A pressure group of women was formed by the youth group in one of our Kalyan communities to work on the issue of sanitation. Girls in this community have been raising their concerns with the local authorities on the insufficient public toilet structures in comparison to the population.

Aim to have Zero Dropouts

Girls from our Andheri community worked on re-enrolments of dropouts and the right to education. Based on the survey results from last year, they were able to re-enroll 6 students in school and 4 students in college. This has been possible due to the persistent work by girls over the years on rights to education.

Girls become independent and gain a deeper understanding of their rights and an insight into the patriarchal society.

The older girls emerge as leaders and ideal role models for the younger program participants thereby inspiring them to carry out community action with the needed optimism.

Girls are empowered with skills such as planning, critical thinking, fundraising, budgeting, networking and problem-solving.

Girls understood field challenges and brainstorm on the solutions which prepares them for the outside world where they learn to face challenges bravely.

Mobile Library

Over 3380 children’s books in seven different languages such as Hindi, English, Urdu, Marathi, Gujarati, Tamil and Telugu are made available to those who enroll themselves for the activities conducted by Vacha or exclusively for the library.

Reach & Impact

Vacha has a large collection of books on/by women which is now housed in Dombivali. This year, we aimed to formalize the existing mobile library programme and set up a permanent library in a poor community in the recently urbanized village of Golavali near Kalyan for underprivileged children and youth. There is no library facility in that area. We decorated the centre and mobilized about the library in the nearby communities and schools which led to the influx of students from the nearby schools who never had an opportunity to read a book.

Books of different genres- storybooks, poetry, science and technology, quiz books, general knowledge, activity books, games, dictionaries, etc. along with children’s magazines and newspapers were included in the library. Our newspaper reading sessions generated considerable interest in adolescents as this was the first time they had read newspapers and played interactive educational games. Many children shared that they loved reading poems, especially, in Hindi. Vacha youth groups played a critical role in driving this program in the center and nearby schools.

Over 7000 books were distributed amongst adolescents from 21 neighborhood schools.

Bal Kishori: Girls in Early Adolescence

A handbook in Hindi on Puberty and Menstruation focusing on breaking the taboo and myths related to girls’ mobility with some worldwide data on health and education of adolescent girls.

Shabda Nishabda

An innovative board game that aims at gathering qualitative information about adolescents, their lifestyle, the role of healthcare and their opinions about issues related to health. This game was designed by Sreya Majumdar from Srishti Institute of Art, Design and Technology, who was interning for her project with Vacha for over 6 months. She was inspired by our program participants and created this board game to facilitate qualitative conversations among girls.
