We, at Vacha are a group of women with varied backgrounds such as social activism, community work, media, research, management, law, writing, finance and teaching.

Our interventions are with intersectionally deprived adolescents in their second decade their lives. They live in poor bastis (commonly referred to as slums). We primarily work with girls and sensitise boys to ensure an effective support system for girls in their families and communities. Vacha also visibalises and advocates for girls and women by participating in various networks and campaigns, organising seminars and conferences and production of publications.

Many lives have been touched, changed or impacted as with the ripple effect, the girls and youth participating in our interventions have assumed agency to bring about change in their families, friends and communities.

Creating impact on future generations

Girls at all Vacha community centres bring out bi-annual newsletters in their respective communities. Programme participants from some communities will release their 25th issue in August 2021.

Older girls emerge as leaders and ideal role models for the younger programme participants thereby inspiring them to carry out community action with the needed optimism.

Girls are empowered with skills such as planning, critical thinking, fund-raising, budgeting, networking and problem-solving.

Girls have advocated and protested against early marriage; continuing education; and scholarship support has further helped them pursue higher education.

Girls who have been part of our Girls' Empowerment Programme have pursued diverse careers in law, social work, para-medical, interior designing, fashion designing, finance and information technology.

Over the years, Vacha has reached out to hundreds of thousands of intersectionally deprived adolescent girls and youth from disadvantaged backgrounds.

Yagna Parmar

Yagna has a Master’s degree in Social Work in which she topped her university course. She has also done a Post Graduate Diploma in career counselling and has developed a career fair programme for girls and youth from the deprived section. She has contributed to the issues of women and girls for the last 14 years. Her dedication and hard work has resulted in her rising from a Junior Social Worker to the level of Projects Director in Vacha. She is currently a Director of Vacha Resource Centre for Women and Girls - a project of Vacha Charitable Trust. Her role involves project management, monitoring the project execution, strategic planning, fund raising and Donor management.

Nischint Hora

Nischint has qualifications in literature, media and women’s studies. She has worked with Doordarshan, the National television channel for eighteen years. She has been with Vacha for close to 25 years. She has extensively contributed to Vacha's Gender Training Programme. Along with Vacha's Founder, Sonal Shukla, she has published a book on Experiencing Girlhood in Slums in Mumbai. She has produced documentaries and publications of Vacha and has worked on the creative expressions of girls. She has also played a major role in managing Vacha's social media channels.

Steffi Fernando

Steffi is a social impact professional and has successfully straddled multiple roles, including designing, monitoring and executing projects. Her experience of close to a decade in working with nonprofits and handling diverse portfolios like programme ideation, implementation, creating detailed monitoring and evaluation indicators, managing grant portfolios, reporting and documentation makes her a multifaceted individual in driving results for the organisation. Steffi has a Master’s degree in Social Work and a Bachelor’s degree in Mass Media with specialisation in Advertising. Additionally, she has a Diploma in personal counselling.

Darshana Shah

A Commerce (Merit holder) and a Law graduate, Darshana has been in the accounting and administration field for the last three decades. She has successfully juggled multiple responsibilities in Vacha. Having a long-standing experience, she is well versed with accounting principles and keeps herself updated with the amendments in Income Tax rules. As a Head of Finance & Administration at Vacha, she manages all the finance and administrative work. She is in charge of regular financial reporting to the Board and the funders. Her willingness to learn and explore has kept her motivated and determined.

Dineshwari Thonse

Dineshwari Thonse has been active in the women’s movement for the last three decades. She holds a Master’s degree in Economics and an M.Phil in English Literature. She is a retired lecturer in Smt. Mittal College of Commerce and a former Trustee at TOUCH – Turning Opportunities for Upliftment and Child Help. Meenal Patel

Meenal Patel, a founder Trustee of Vacha, is a well known actor in theatre and television in Hindi and Gujarati, including street theatre. She brings in her creative ideas in developing resources in form of songs and poems on girls and women’s issues.

Sonal Shukla

Sonal Shukla, the chief founder of Vacha Charitable Trust has a background in Gandhian Education, Literature and Aesthetics. She has been actively involved with Indian Women’s Movement from its inception. She has written extensively on women's issues and has been writing a regular coloumn on women related topics in Gujarati newspaper for over four decades. She is also on the editorial boards of the interdisciplinary journal published at Montreal , "Girlhood Studies", and of the quarterly, "Quest in Education", published by Indian Council of Basic Education, Mumbai. Sonal Shukla is the Hon. Director of Vacha Charitable Trust and oversees the governance of Vacha, fund raising and guides the project team. She has been a recipient of the various awards for her work on women's issues over the years. In 2021, Sonal Shukla has been recognised as a Feminist Icon in India by Friedrich Ebert Stiftung, for their Gender Justice hub in Asia and has also received the Mahatma award initiated by the Aditya Birla Group for her work in Gender Equality.

Dr. Vibhuti Patel

Dr. Vibhuti Patel has been active in the women’s movement for the last three decades and is a well known Economist. Dr. Vibhuti Patel is Ph.D. in Economics, University of Mumbai. She is a retired Professor and Head of Post Graduate Department of Economics, SNDT Women’s University, Mumbai. She is also a retired Professor and Chairperson of Advanced Centre for Women’s Studies, School of Development Studies at Tata Institute of Social Sciences (TISS). She was awarded a Visiting Fellowship to the London School of Economics and Politcal Science from Association of Commonwealth Universities, UK in 1992-93. Dr. Patel has authored and co-authored 12 books; edited and co-edited 9 books and contributed over 100 papers as chapters in various books edited by others. She has been a Paper Coordinator for ‘Women and Economics’ (20 modules) for MA Women’s Studies Course. She has written extensively on gender and women's rights. As an Expert Committee member of the Working Group on Discrimination against Women and Girls of United Nations Human Rights Council, she contributed to prepare a Report on Women's Human Rights in Changing World of Work, 2020. Dr. Patel is on the board of many social and women’s organizations. She is a receipient of many awards and recognitions for her work in various disciplines.

Dr. Veena Devasthali

Dr. Veena Devasthali has been Professor and Head at the Department of Economics, SNDT Women’s University, Mumbai. With a Ph.D. from Indian Institute of Technology (IIT), Mumbai, her areas of core competency are Macro Economics, Development Economics, Indian Economy, Industrial Economics and Labor Economics. She has been on various important committees for academic development and management. She had an early association with Women’s Movement and the issues, it has taken up, for gender justice.
